## Key points to cover
- Be upfront that Notion already has an "Export to PDF" and "Export to Markdown & CSV" option — this post's job is to explain why the Markdown route produces a better, more editable Word file than the PDF route for documents that need further editing.
- Cover nested sub-pages honestly: Notion's Markdown export creates separate files for sub-pages rather than inlining them, so flag that as a real limitation to plan around, not something the converter step fixes.
- Keep scope realistic: toggle lists and databases are Notion-specific concepts with no Word equivalent, so explain what they degrade to (expanded text, a plain table) rather than implying perfect fidelity.
